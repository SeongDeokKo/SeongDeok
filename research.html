Characteristics-based reversals: Exploiting the gap between predicted and realized returns

Seongdeok Ko

Finance Research Letters, Volume 85 (Part C), November 2025, Article 108081

ScienceDirect DOI
Abstract

This paper studies short-term return reversals implied by the mismatch between characteristic-predicted return ranks and realized return ranks. A long–short strategy exploiting large decile-level divergences earns strong gross returns, but profitability is concentrated in small, illiquid, high-volatility stocks where transaction costs meaningfully limit implementability. A within-industry peer-based refinement improves net performance by filtering noise and targeting more comparable firms.

Abstract

Using BOJ ETF purchases as a natural experiment, the paper shows that when interventions are predictable, trading volume in Nikkei 225 futures spikes during the lunch break—when the cash market is closed—while prices remain stable.
