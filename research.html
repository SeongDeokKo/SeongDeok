Characteristics-based reversals: Exploiting the gap between predicted and realized returns

Seongdeok Ko

Finance Research Letters, Volume 85 (Part C), November 2025

DOI ScienceDirect
Abstract

This paper studies short-term return reversals implied by the mismatch between characteristic-predicted return ranks and realized return ranks. A long–short strategy exploiting large decile-level divergences earns strong gross returns, but profitability is concentrated in small, illiquid, high-volatility stocks where transaction costs meaningfully limit implementability.

Market anticipation and intraday trading: Evidence from BOJ ETF purchases

Seongdeok Ko

Finance Research Letters, Volume 91, March 2026

DOI ScienceDirect
Abstract

Using the Bank of Japan’s (BOJ) equity ETF purchase program as a natural experiment, we estimate intervention probabilities from intraday returns and examine their effects on Nikkei 225 futures. We find that markets internalize expected policy actions through execution timing and liquidity provision rather than price adjustment.
